Hathor Temple

Late Ptolematic - Roman Periods

Christian OKeefe

Introduction

Hathor Temple (formerly Temple of Tentyra) falls within the Dendera Temple complex just a short travel from modern Qena, Egypt. It was constructed in dedication to the Ancient Egyptian Goddess Hathor; the goddess of motherhood, joy, and feminimity. While the standing structure as it is today was not built until the Late Plotematic period, the site itself dates back to the Middle Kingdom. Since the temple's construction it has seen numerous renovations and changes. Throughout its excavation there has been multiple findings that have had made major impacts on our current thinking of Ancient Egyptian culture, religion, and society.
